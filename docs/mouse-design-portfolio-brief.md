# Mouse Product Design Portfolio Brief

## Project Essence

这是一个以趴握舒适性为核心的鼠标形态设计。项目从大量发散手绘开始，经历仿生方向、曲面建模、3D 打印验证和多轮修正，最终收束为一个圆润、高后背、带侧面渐消纹理的白色鼠标。

作品集里最值得强调的不是“做了一个鼠标”，而是“通过手绘、建模和实体打印逐步发现形态与人机关系的问题，并把发散的仿生灵感转化成更克制、更可用的产品语言”。

## Design Narrative

### Problem

Most conventional mice do not fully support the palm, especially around the root of the fingers. A design that only looks smooth can still fail if the front angle leaves too little room for finger force or if the body becomes visually heavy.

### Goal

Create a relatively rounded, comfortable palm-grip mouse that gives the hand a clear support surface while preserving enough space and angle for active finger movement.

### Design Direction

The early direction explored biomimicry from a ladybird: a compact arched body, resilient feeling, and high-backed shell. Later iterations moved away from literal biomimicry and kept the useful parts: the soft shell volume, arched support logic, and flowing side lines.

### Process Arc

- **Divergent sketching:** Explored palm rests, front-end shapes, thin-shell forms, unusual silhouettes, centerlines, side arcs, and top outlines.
- **Direction forming:** Realized one product cannot carry every stylistic feature, so the project narrowed toward a rounded, comfortable palm-grip mouse built from circles and ellipses.
- **Early modeling:** Tested a ladybird-inspired high-arch structure, but the first model felt too thick and heavy.
- **Curve breakthrough:** A reference with a central opening and double-curve line suggested a more elegant side structure; extracting part of an ellipse and blending the remainder became the basis for the flowing body line.
- **Digital iteration:** Rounded the rear to better fit the hand, corrected twisted side surfaces, refined the scroll-wheel trench, and added streamlined side textures.
- **Physical validation:** 3D printing exposed issues that were not obvious on screen: the opening was too large, the button area looked fragile, the front slope was too steep, and the rear volume was too bulky.
- **Final refinement:** Removed the oversized opening and steep front angle, kept the outer arc, fading texture, scroll-wheel detail, and bottom parting line, then added side buttons and fading side surfaces.

## Final Design Features

- **Raised rear support:** The back is higher than a typical mouse, supporting the root of the fingers and palm while leaving space for finger force.
- **Elliptical surface construction:** The top surface uses half-ellipse logic and blended curves to keep the body unified and soft.
- **Closed, rounded shell:** Removing the large opening made the product feel stronger, cleaner, and more coherent.
- **Fading side texture:** The side detail enriches the parting surfaces, produces stronger light and shadow, and adds a tactile cue for where the hand rests.
- **Scroll-wheel groove:** The groove organizes the front detail and gives the wheel area a more deliberate mechanical expression.
- **Pill-shaped side button:** The side button is twisted and softened to match the shell language, with a subtle concave cue for touch guidance.
- **Bottom parting line:** The lower seam creates a clear shadow boundary and gives the white body more visual definition.

## Portfolio Copy Options

### Short Case Study Intro

This mouse concept began as a broad form exploration and gradually narrowed into a palm-grip ergonomic study. The final design uses a raised rear shell to support the hand, a softened elliptical top surface for visual unity, and fading side textures that create both light-shadow detail and tactile guidance.

### Process Caption

Early sketches explored palm rests, thin shells, side arcs, and unusual mouse silhouettes. After testing a ladybird-inspired arched volume, the project shifted from literal biomimicry toward a cleaner ergonomic body shaped by blended ellipses and flowing side lines.

### Prototype Caption

The 3D print revealed problems that the digital model hid: the front angle was too steep, the opening made the button area look fragile, and the rear body felt too bulky. These findings drove the final closed shell, smoother side surfaces, and more controlled support volume.

### Detail Caption

The fading side texture is not only decoration. It breaks up the white surface, creates stronger shadow transitions, and subtly marks the hand-contact area. The wheel groove, side button, and bottom seam repeat the same soft, flowing design language.
